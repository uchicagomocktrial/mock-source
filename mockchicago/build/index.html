What do we do?

At the beginning of each year, the American Mock Trial Association releases new case materials. We spend the most of the year debating the same case with other schools around the country, then switch it up with a new case at the National Championship Tournament in April.

Debate

Join the team as an attorney to hone your legal skills. Attorneys win points for the team by making speeches, examining witnesses, and arguing objections.

Act

Join the team as a witness to work on your improv and show off your acting chops. Witnesses win points by being likeable and realistic. Grieving widows should be sad, and theme park mascots should be funny.

Compete

Test what you've prepared against some of the best mock trial teams in the country. Our rounds are judged by practicing judges and attorneys in real courthouses.

Win

We regularly place first or second in the region, and are among the top 20 teams in the nation. Join the team, work hard, and wait for the fan mail to roll in.

The Great Chicago Fire

Invitational Tournament

The Great Chicago Fire is the University's invitational mock trial tournament. Congratulations to the winner of the inaugural competition--The Ohio State University! The second annual Great Chicago Fire invitational will be held from January 16th and January 17th, 2016 at the Dirksen Federal Courthouse in Downtown Chicago. Unlike most mock trial tournaments, the Great Chicago Fire will feature bracket style elimination .

We will be sending out invitations starting in the summer of 2015. To learn more, please contact the tournament committee.

Great Chicago Fire Elimination Rules

Mockers will compete as a school, instead of as independent teams. There will be sixteen schools at the tournament; each school will have two teams. First round, each school will be paired with another school. For example, if School 1 was paired with School 2, each of School 1’s teams (1A and 1B) would the corresponding team from School 2 (2A and 2B, respectively). For each pairing of schools, there are two trials, each with three ballots, for a total of six ballots (1A v.1B & 2A v. 2B, three ballots each). From these six ballots, one of the schools is determined the winner of the pairing, and the other school is eliminated, and moves on to the next round where they compete against another school that won in the first round, until only two schools are left in the bracket.

When a school loses a round, they drop into a normal tournament with weighted pairings, like a standard AMTA style tournament, so every team will play a full eight rounds. The champion of the regular tournament will be awarded 3rd place.
